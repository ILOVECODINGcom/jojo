About Me

I am a 19-year-old first-year student at University College London (UCL). I have a passion for traveling, exploring new places, and immersing myself in diverse cultures. Staying active through sports helps me maintain a balanced and healthy lifestyle. Cooking is another joy of mine, where I can unwind and experiment with flavors, creating meals to share with friends and family.

At this stage in my life, I am eager to embrace new experiences, both academically and personally. Whether it's discovering new destinations, improving my skills, or meeting people from around the world, I aim to make the most of my time at university while growing into the best version of myself.

Voluntary activities

  • In high school, I initiated and organized a volunteer program dedicated to rescuing stray cats. Working alongside fellow students, we created awareness campaigns, arranged for veterinary care, and found loving homes for the cats in need. This experience not only deepened my love for animals but also taught me the importance of teamwork, leadership, and compassion in making a tangible difference.

  • During the summer, I participated in a marine conservation initiative on the beaches of Sanya, China. I joined efforts to clean up the coastline, collecting litter to protect the marine environment and preserve the natural beauty of the area. This experience strengthened my awareness of environmental issues and my commitment to sustainability.

  • In high school, I organized a charitable initiative where fresh flowers were exchanged for items free of charge. The collected items were then donated to left-behind children, providing them with essential supplies and small comforts. This experience allowed me to combine creativity with compassion, fostering a sense of community and support for those in need.

  • While in Tibet, I had the unique experience of helping local herders tend to their cattle. This opportunity allowed me to immerse myself in the region's culture, appreciate the simplicity of pastoral life, and gain a deeper understanding of the bond between people and nature.

Photos of volunteer activities

Stray cats

Feeding them

Stray cats

Feeding them

The mother cat gave birth to a litter of kittens at our high school, but unfortunately, one of the kittens did not survive the cold and froze to death. Witnessing this made us realize the importance of taking action to protect the animals around us. Together, we worked to provide a safe and warm environment for the remaining kittens, ensuring they were healthy and cared for.

Later, we found loving adoption homes for the surviving kittens, giving them a chance at a better life. To prevent future litters and protect the mother cat's health, we arranged for her to be spayed. Afterward, we brought her back to the school, where we placed her in a cozy cat shelter and continued to feed and care for her. This experience taught us the value of compassion and responsibility in making a difference, no matter how small.

Marine conservation

Beautiful ocean

We spent the entire afternoon cleaning up the litter scattered along the coastline. Every piece of trash we picked up felt like a small step toward protecting the ocean and preserving its natural beauty. The effort was tiring but deeply rewarding as we watched the shore gradually transform back to its pristine state.

By the time we took this photo, it was already evening. The golden hues of the sunset reflected off the calm waves, as if the ocean itself was showing gratitude for our hard work. Standing there, with the cool breeze and the sound of the waves, we felt a deep connection to nature and a renewed sense of responsibility to protect it.

Bartering

Helping left-behind children

We spent two days preparing for this event, using flowers we purchased ourselves to exchange for items from classmates and teachers. The idea was simple yet meaningful: through bartering, we collected supplies and essentials that could be donated to left-behind children, offering them a small token of care and support.

By sharing the beauty of flowers, we hoped to spread love and hope to those in need. The event not only brought people together but also reminded us of the power of compassion and small actions in creating a positive impact on others' lives.

Grazing

On the plateau

While traveling in Tibet, I happened to meet a young herder boy on the plateau. He was tending to a group of cattle by himself, and seeing his dedication piqued my curiosity. After a brief conversation, I decided to help him for the afternoon, stepping into his world of vast grasslands and quiet companionship with nature.

For the next few hours, I experienced the simplicity and serenity of herding cattle on the plateau. The mountains stretched endlessly on the horizon, and the cool breeze carried with it a sense of calm. This unexpected encounter became one of the most memorable parts of my journey, giving me a deeper appreciation for the life and culture of the Tibetan highlands.
